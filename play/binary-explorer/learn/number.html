Counting in Binary

Here, we will learn how to count using binary.

In binary, the different places in the number have different values. Staring on the right, the first digit represents the 1's place. The second digit from the right represents the 2's place. The third digit represents the 4's place. The next four places are the 8's, 16's, 32's, and 64's.

Let's look at the number 1101 in binary. Starting on the right, we see that the 1's place is turned on. That means our number contains has a 1. The 2's place is turned off, so our number does not contain a 2. The next two places are turned on, so our number contains 4 and 8. To find out what our binary number is, we add together the values of the places which are turned on, which are 1, 4, and 8.

1 + 4 + 8 = 13

That means 1101 in binary equals 13.
